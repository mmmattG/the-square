# Lore Direction

## Premise

`the-square` is a controlled company facility, not a free settlement. The engineer is confined inside a deliberately minimal production enclosure and is expected to convert company-provided raw inputs into quota materials.

The company never acknowledges the confinement as unethical or unusual. Its voice stays upbeat, procedural, and delusionally optimistic, as if the engineer is participating in a normal orientation and enjoying improved workplace accommodations.

## Company Framing

- The company is cruel, but it presents itself as helpful and professional.
- It feigns ignorance about the obvious horror of the situation.
- It does not explain the real purpose of the quota materials.
- It treats their value as obvious, classified, and not the engineer's concern.
- It frames additional square footage as a privilege earned through useful output.

## Terminology Direction

- `Facility` is the normal company term for the square and its production space.
- `Accommodations` is used occasionally in a joking-but-serious corporate tone when the company pretends the engineer is being well looked after.
- `Quota material` is the preferred fiction for science packs.
- Different science packs are different quota products, not discoveries or inventions.

## Ingress Fiction

Ingress is company provisioning, not local mining and not interplanetary shipping.

- Raw resources are delivered by the company into the facility through approved ingress points.
- Ingress points are compact point sources, not long visible lines from outside the map.
- The player is expected to reposition ingress as needed within company rules.
- The company controls where and when raw input is dispensed.

## Ingress Visual Direction

- Ingress should look like a circular hatch or manhole-like floor aperture.
- When idle, the hatch is closed and inert.
- In alt mode, the assigned resource should still be visible to the player.
- When active, material rises via a magnetic or antigravity field rather than a mechanical lift.
- The company hardware should feel far more advanced than anything the engineer can build.
- The aesthetic target is clinical but worn from repeated corporate reuse.

## Expansion Fiction

- Square expansion is not organic growth.
- The engineer produces quota materials for the company.
- When a major output threshold is met, the company authorizes expanded workspace.
- Expanded space is framed as earned privilege, not entitlement.
- Lore beats should trigger at major science-band transitions rather than every ring expansion.

## Narrative Delivery

- Opening lore should be explicit and short.
- The first pass should use brief sequential text lines.
- The tone should resemble corporate orientation messaging.
- The company voice should sound routine, positive, and detached from the engineer's reality.
- Milestone lore should appear at major science-band completions, especially when the last expansion of a band is completed.
- Any later art treatment such as company cards, portraits, or a radio/speaker motif is optional follow-up work, not required for the first implementation.

## Writing Rules

- Do not acknowledge the ethical problem directly.
- Do not break tone with jokes that admit the cruelty plainly.
- Do not describe science as knowledge, discovery, or invention.
- Do describe quota products as valuable, opaque, and important to the company.
- Keep messages brief, hostile in implication, and professionally upbeat on the surface.
